No hype here — this is about what actually worked in a real task.
Audience: Non-technical operators. Outcome focus: Reduce rework. Framing angle: Pitfall review.

I ran a compact before/after validation around one specific task and tracked three things: turnaround speed, quality stability, and rework load.
Reference source: @pascal_bornet https://nitter.net/pascal_bornet/status/2031263823030825047#m

Here is the core takeaway: the value does not come from "using AI", it comes from making the workflow reusable.
I defined scope and success criteria first, then executed a narrow workflow with lightweight human review checkpoints.

The practical result was clearer first drafts, fewer revision loops, and faster decisions on what to keep or discard.
If you want to reuse this, start with one high-frequency task, keep constraints explicit, and iterate weekly based on outcomes.

Boundary: this works best for recurring tasks with clear evaluation criteria.
